The tokens beneath everything.

FAQ accordion

<details> native · plus/minus affordance
When will my order ship?
Orders placed before 2 p.m. EST ship the same business day from our New York warehouse. Outerwear made to order may take 10–14 days; the product page will tell you when.
How do I know my size?
Each product page has a size guide with body and garment measurements. If you're between sizes, we've noted which way we recommend you go — and a human on our team will answer a fit question by email within a day.
What's your return policy?
Thirty days, free, no form to fill in. Print a label, drop it at any carrier. Refunds land in 3–5 business days from the moment we scan the package back in.
Do you repair pieces that were damaged?
Yes — outerwear and bags come with a lifetime repair guarantee. Email us a photo; we'll send a prepaid label and have the workshop stitch it back together.
Where are things made?
Mostly in Portugal, where our workshops are. A handful of pieces are made in Italy (knitwear) and Japan (canvas bags). The tag inside each piece tells you.

Journal/Craft/Issue №07
Craft · Issue №07 · 8 min read

In the Porto workshop, where the Linden Coat is made.

Helena Costa
Editor · Nov 12, 2025
Hero photograph · Manuel Almeida

Eleven people, four sewing tables, and a kettle that is always on. Teresa has been here for twenty-two years. She does not look up when we come in.

The Linden Coat begins as a flat rectangle of double-faced wool, cut the night before. Teresa walks her hand across it the way a baker walks a hand across dough — looking for a weave that has slipped, a thread that has caught.

"You don't rush wool. Wool has been waiting a long time to be a coat. You can wait too."

The workshop closes at six. Teresa will finish the coat tomorrow.

About

Small runs, long use.

Atelier began in 2014 with three people, a rented studio in Porto, and one coat. That coat is still in the line — it's the Linden — and the studio is still in Porto, though the rent went up.

We make clothes in small runs, mostly of natural fibers, mostly in Portugal. We don't chase seasons. We fix what breaks. We tell you where each piece is made, who made it, and how long they've been doing this kind of work.

Studio photograph

That's it, really. We'd rather stock less, and stand behind it.
